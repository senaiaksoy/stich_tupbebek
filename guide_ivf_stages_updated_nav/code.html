The Clinical Stages of In Vitro Fertilization

A rigorous examination of the four foundational phases of Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART). This guide outlines the physiological and laboratory processes involved in a standard IVF cycle.

Medical Reviewer

Dr. Senai Aksoy

01

Ovarian Stimulation

The initial phase involves the administration of gonadotropins to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple mature follicles, rather than the single egg typically released during a natural menstrual cycle.

  • Hormonal monitoring via blood analysis (Estradiol levels).
  • Transvaginal ultrasound to track follicular growth and diameter.
  • Final maturation induction using a "trigger" injection (hCG or GnRH agonist).

Technical Insight

Transvaginal ultrasound visualization is critical for timing the retrieval procedure to ensure maximum oocyte viability.

02

Egg Retrieval (Oocyte Pick-Up)

Approximately 34 to 36 hours post-trigger, the retrieval is performed under light sedation. A ultrasound-guided needle is used to aspirate the follicular fluid containing the oocytes.

"The procedure typically involves a minor surgical intervention lasting 20–30 minutes. Patients may experience mild cramping post-procedure, though recovery is generally rapid."
03

Fertilization and Embryo Culture

Once retrieved, oocytes are evaluated for maturity and fertilized using either conventional insemination or Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI).

Insemination

Sperm and egg are placed in a culture dish to allow natural penetration.

ICSI

A single sperm is directly injected into the oocyte, used for specific male factor indications.

Following fertilization, embryos are cultured in an incubator for 3 to 5 days until they reach the cleavage or blastocyst stage.

04

Embryo Transfer

The final clinical step involves placing one or more selected embryos into the patient's uterus using a thin, flexible catheter. This procedure is generally painless and performed without sedation.

task_alt

Blastocyst Transfer

Typically occurring on day 5, favoring synchronization with the uterine environment.

task_alt

Luteal Support

Progesterone supplementation is often prescribed to support the endometrial lining.

The Importance of Clinical Precision

Each stage of IVF requires rigorous environmental controls and clinical expertise. Success rates are multifactorial, influenced by maternal age, etiology of infertility, and laboratory conditions.
